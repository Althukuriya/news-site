  In a dramatic late-night development on April 8, 2026, the United States,
  Iran, and Israel announced a two-week ceasefire to pause the five-week-old
  conflict that has roiled the Middle East and sent global oil prices soaring.


  President Donald Trump hailed the agreement in a social media post, calling it "a big day for World Peace!" The truce comes after weeks of U.S. and Israeli strikes on Iranian targets, Iranian retaliatory actions, and threats from Trump that escalated tensions dramatically, including warnings that failure to reach a deal could lead to severe consequences.


  Key terms of the ceasefire include Iran reopening the **Strait of Hormuz** — a vital chokepoint for global oil transport — in exchange for a suspension of U.S. and allied attacks. Negotiations between the U.S. and Iran are scheduled to begin on Friday in Islamabad, Pakistan.


  ### Lingering Tensions and Complications


  Despite the announcement, the ceasefire's implementation faces immediate challenges:


  * Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu stated that the deal does **not** cover operations in Lebanon, where Israeli strikes continued, causing significant casualties in Beirut and the Bekaa Valley.

  * Confusion persists over the exact application to the Strait of Hormuz, with some reports of ongoing restrictions and retaliatory actions.

  * Iran has launched ballistic missiles at several targets even after the ceasefire took hold, and explosions were reported on Iranian islands, prompting further accusations and counter-strikes in the region.

  * Russia and China vetoed a watered-down UN resolution aimed at reopening the strait, adding diplomatic complexity.


  Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth and Joint Chiefs officials held a briefing to clarify the U.S. position, emphasizing that the pause is conditional on Iranian compliance.


  ### Global Impact


  The conflict had already disrupted global energy markets, with oil prices spiking before plunging back under $100 per barrel on news of the ceasefire. The closure of the Strait of Hormuz had ripple effects on fertilizer prices and farming in the U.S. and beyond.


  Civilian impacts have been severe, with rising death tolls reported in affected areas, including Lebanon. An American journalist kidnapped in Iraq was released amid the broader regional turmoil.


  ### Reactions


  * Democrats in the U.S. have called for accountability over the administration's handling of threats during the crisis.

  * International observers, including the Vatican, condemned escalatory rhetoric used in the lead-up to the deal.

  * Analysts describe the framework as a fragile starting point, with some calling it a "catastrophically bad" basis for long-term negotiations.


  This ceasefire provides a short-term breathing room, but experts warn that without successful talks in Islamabad, hostilities could resume quickly. Markets are watching closely for stability in energy supplies, while world leaders urge all parties to exercise restraint
