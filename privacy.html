Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2025

ByteBulletin ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains how we handle information when you visit our news website.

Information We Collect

We collect minimal data on this static website:

  • Local Storage: Your bookmarks and dark mode preference are stored locally on your device using browser localStorage.
  • Anonymous Analytics: We may use basic page view counting for statistical purposes.
  • No Tracking Cookies: We do not use cookies for tracking or advertising purposes.

How We Use Your Data

Since ByteBulletin is a static website powered by a JSON-based content management system:

  • We do not collect personal information like names, emails, or IP addresses
  • We do not share any data with third parties
  • All content is served directly from static files

Local Storage & Bookmarks

Your saved articles and reading preferences are stored exclusively in your browser's local storage. This data never leaves your device. You can clear your bookmarks at any time by:

  • Using the browser's developer tools to clear local storage
  • Clearing your browser cache and site data

Third-Party Services

Our website uses the following third-party services:

  • Google Fonts: For typography. May collect IP addresses.
  • FontAwesome: For icons. May collect usage data.
  • OneSignal (optional): For push notifications if you opt-in. You can unsubscribe anytime.

Children's Privacy

Our website is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children.

Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Clear your bookmarks at any time via browser settings
  • Disable push notifications in your browser preferences
  • Use the website with JavaScript disabled (limited functionality)

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy occasionally. Any changes will be posted on this page.
